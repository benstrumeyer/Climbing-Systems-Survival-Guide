# Climbing Systems

A personal climbing coach focused on safety systems, technique, and training progressions — with the ultimate goal of climbing **El Capitan**.

## Vision

An app that literally teaches me the ropes: ingests curated knowledge from books and the web, then acts as a coach that generates lessons, drills, and training plans for outdoor climbing. Safety-first, systems-focused, and tuned to my current level.

## Scope

Covers the full spectrum of climbing disciplines and the systems that keep you alive outdoors:

- **Lead climbing** — clipping, falling, rope management, runout assessment
- **Trad climbing** — gear placement, rack building, cam/nut selection, removing gear
- **Bouldering** — movement, power, projecting, spotting, pad placement
- **Multi-pitch** — transitions, belay stations, following, simul-climbing
- **Big wall** — aid climbing, hauling, portaledges, sleeping on the wall
- **Anchors** — SERENE/ERNEST, equalization, redundancy, natural vs. fixed
- **Knots** — figure-eight, clove hitch, munter, prusik, alpine butterfly, bowline on a bight, etc.
- **Rappelling** — backups, extensions, stuck ropes, contingency anchors
- **Self-rescue** — escaping the belay, ascending a rope, hauling a partner
- **Training** — hangboard, campus, antagonists, endurance, periodization for big walls

## Goal: El Capitan

The North Star is climbing El Cap. The app should work backwards from that goal and produce:

- A realistic multi-phase training plan (strength, endurance, aid skills, wall fitness)
- A systems curriculum (everything I need to know to not die on the wall)
- Progression checkpoints (routes/skills to tick off before committing to a wall route)
- Route-specific prep (The Nose, Freerider, Zodiac, etc.)

## Current Level

- **V9 boulderer** — strong on movement and power
- Need to build: rope skills, trad systems, endurance, big wall systems, aid climbing

## Knowledge Sources

Content will come from **user-directed sources only** — no scraping the open web blindly. Sources TBD, but likely a mix of:

- Authoritative climbing books (e.g. *Freedom of the Hills*, John Long's anchor books, *Big Wall Climbing* by Middendorf, etc.)
- Curated web resources (AMGA, trusted guide sites, specific YouTube channels)
- Possibly scraped + processed into a structured knowledge base

The ingestion pipeline and the exact source list will be defined as the project evolves.

## Coach Behavior (rough idea)

- Generates lessons on demand ("teach me trad anchors")
- Builds progressive curricula toward a goal
- Quizzes on systems and knots
- Recommends next drills / next routes based on current level
- Always prioritizes safety and cites its sources
